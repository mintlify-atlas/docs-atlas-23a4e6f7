This playbook uses **Replit** as the focal account for a customer expansion exercise. Replit migrated from Docusaurus to Mintlify and derives measurable value from docs-as-code workflows, contributor collaboration, and documentation automation. This page covers how Replit uses Mintlify today, prioritized expansion opportunities, the barriers that may slow adoption, what you need to learn to qualify each opportunity, and the concrete signals that indicate Replit is ready to expand.

## How Replit uses Mintlify today

Replit runs a Git-backed, version-controlled documentation workflow that lets anyone on the team contribute. Since migrating from Docusaurus, **contributors grew from 2 to 10+**, each pull request **saves ~30–60 minutes**, and sales reports confirm that customers find information easily via search.

Key capabilities Replit has adopted:

- **Vale linting** for consistent prose quality
- **Automated changelogs** (~90% reduction in manual effort)
- **AI-assisted writing** for faster content production
- **Diátaxis-style structure** (tutorials, how-tos, reference) for organized content
- **LLM-friendly docs index** at [docs.replit.com](https://docs.replit.com), including an `llms.txt` file already in place for agent discoverability

Replit Agent and other AI features are fully documented on Mintlify, making Replit a strong candidate for analytics and AI-related expansion features.

## Why adoption hasn't happened yet

Adoption can lag for several reasons: a post-migration consolidation phase, an ownership gap, or a team prioritizing content quality and accessibility over new product features. Below are the most likely factors for Replit.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Post-migration focus">
    The team is likely in an "it works, don't touch it" phase after the Docusaurus→Mintlify move. Expanding from 2 to 10+ contributors is a significant workflow change to absorb before taking on additional product features.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Ownership gap">
    The engineering effort that drove the migration may be complete, with no single owner yet designated for the next phase — such as enabling AI features on the docs site.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Content and accessibility first">
    Replit's [documentation transformation blog post](https://www.mintlify.com/blog/behind-replits-documentation-transformation) emphasizes rewriting for "vibe coders" and AI agents, and improving internal processes. User-facing AI chat on docs may not have been in scope for the initial project.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## What to learn to qualify

Use the following questions to qualify each expansion opportunity before bringing it to Replit.

| Topic | What to find out | Qualifies |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Support / forum volume** | How many inbound questions are already answered in docs? | AI Assistant ROI and deflection potential |
| **Docs ownership** | Who owns docs post-migration? Is responsibility diffuse? | Readiness for a dedicated expansion owner |
| **Language mix** | Is there meaningful non-English traffic? | AI translations opportunity |
| **AI tool usage** | Do users pull Replit docs via Cursor, Windsurf, or MCP? | MCP/skill and discoverability value |
| **Seats and plan** | Current seat count and Enterprise plan usage? | Seat and tier expansion |
| **Agent / Assistant usage** | What is AI/agent traffic volume? Are Assistant citations poor? | Analytics for AI/agent traffic and Assistant usage |
| **Playground limits** | Current usage levels and any rate-limit friction? | Paid playground or capacity upsell |

<Tip>
  Prioritize learning about **support volume** and **Agent/Assistant usage** first — these map directly to the two highest-value opportunities (AI Assistant and analytics) and are the easiest to validate with publicly visible data.
</Tip>

## Signals of expansion readiness

Watch for these signals to time the expansion conversation well:

- **Onboarding push**: A public focus on improving onboarding means docs and AI are already on the team's radar.
- **International growth**: Localization efforts or rising non-English user numbers open the AI translations conversation.
- **New docs or DevRel hire**: A dedicated owner almost always signals appetite for new features and tooling.
- **Repeat questions in docs**: Forum or support questions that are already answered in the docs indicate AI Assistant would deflect a meaningful volume.
- **High search failure rate**: If Mintlify analytics show poor findability, bring that data directly to the conversation.
- **High AI/agent traffic or poor Assistant citations**: Sustained agent traffic or Assistant conversations with low-quality citations create a natural fit for Mintlify's AI/agent analytics.
- **Playground or playbook requests**: Asks for capacity, streaming, or agent playbooks signal readiness for paid capacity or professional services.

<Note>
  You don't need all signals to be present. A single strong signal — such as a new DevRel hire or a documented onboarding initiative — is often enough to open the conversation.
</Note>
