Expansion revenue at Mintlify is tied directly to how deeply customers adopt the platform. This page covers the specific levers — seat growth, AI credit consumption, tier upgrades, and more — and lays out a repeatable framework for activating them across your customer base.

## Expansion levers

Levers are tied to pricing and product: seats, AI credits, tier upgrades, Enterprise features, professional services, analytics, and reference/vertical plays.

### Three core pricing levers

| Trigger | Lever | CS action |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Contributor count approaching seat limit | **Seat expansion** — Pro: **5** dashboard members vs Hobby: **1**; Enterprise: unlimited DEV Community | Track before they hit the wall; don't wait for the customer to hit the ceiling. |
| Monthly AI usage trending toward ceiling | **AI credit overages** — Pro/Enterprise include **250** AI credits; overages **$0.25** each; tiered packages available | Monitor trajectory; move customers onto tiered packages before surprise overage bills hurt sentiment at renewal. |
| Second contributor, need for preview deployments, or password protection | **Hobby → Pro → Enterprise**: largest jump is Hobby → Pro | Use these CS-detectable signals to start an upgrade conversation. |

### Additional levers

- **Enterprise features & security**: SSO, RBAC, audit logs, SLA; required for larger ACV.
- **Seats & workspaces**: More editor seats, more repos/sites (multi-repo); teams expand as docs ownership broadens (PMs, support, SEO).
- **Pro services / migration & custom frontend**: Migration, integration, white-label (e.g. "Your docs, your frontend, our content engine") as an enterprise entry point.
- **Analytics & observability**: Once in-house, premium analytics that surface where docs cause agent failures; sell as enterprise add-on.
- **Reference / vertical plays**: Convert wins (Perplexity, Replit, Vercel, Fidelity) into vertical collateral and solution packs for GTM.

### Planning ahead

<Tip>
  Expansion plays will keep evolving. Today's levers will change and new ones will appear. Stay close to the product roadmap and use customer conversations to understand their evolving needs so that when new features ship, customers already see how they fit.
</Tip>

## How to systematically drive expansion

Use the framework below to drive expansion systematically across customer accounts.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion icon="gauge" title="Step 1: Instrument and operationalize">
    - Put triggers into a **dashboard CS looks at daily**, not a monthly report.
    - Have each trigger **auto-create a task** for the CSM assigned to that account.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion icon="sitemap" title="Step 2: Segment and funnel">
    Segment accounts and run a repeatable funnel so expansion is systematic.

    - **Segment** customers (PLG / Mid-Market / Enterprise).
    - **Land**: Accelerate time-to-value through repeatable playbooks and templates.
    - **Adopt**: Measure platform usage such as editor activity, Assistant queries, and Agent usage.
    - **Expand**: Surface in-product offers at defined thresholds (e.g. 80% of AI credits, "Invite teammates to enable AI assistant for the whole team").
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion icon="calendar" title="Step 3: Expansion cadence (EBRs as strategic conversations)">
    Run **EBRs (Executive / Business Reviews)** (for example, at 90 days before renewal) for mid-market and enterprise customers. Use these sessions for **strategic recommendations** that inspire customers and **unlock new or creative use cases and automation** on the platform. Surface expansion opportunities (e.g. API playground adoption, AI agent usage, search metrics) in the context of "here's what you could do next" and trials (e.g. Agent Workflows for select teams) to prove value quickly.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion icon="message" title="Step 4: Conversation framing">
    Frame conversations around value, not just upsell. The expansion conversation should never be about price; frame it around **what the customer is leaving on the table**.

    <Tip>
      **Example script (AI credits):** "I can see your AI Assistant usage is approaching your monthly limit: the readers hitting your docs are getting value from it. Let me show you what the next volume tier unlocks."
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion icon="users" title="Step 5: Cross-functional alignment">
    Ensure effective partnership across departments. Identify the proper resource and schedule internal syncs for additional knowledge transfer as needed.

    <Info>
      There are different ways to partner effectively across departments.

      **Sales:** Partner on co-selling add-ons (seats, AI packages, Enterprise features) when CS identifies expansion-ready accounts. Auto-create an expansion opportunity in your CRM when usage crosses defined thresholds and populate with relevant context so Sales can follow up.

      **Product:** Align on beta features and roadmap (e.g. AI observability, analytics). Share what customers are asking for and what would unlock new use cases; use expansion conversations to tease upcoming capabilities where relevant.
    </Info>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion icon="chart-line" title="Step 6: Measure and refine">
    Track expansion ARR so you know what's working. Share closed won knowledge across teams to develop and learn best practices.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Defining success

Define desired benchmarks and criteria for success. Update and iterate as needed. Examples:

- **Target**: Expansion revenue as **30%+** of ARR.
- **Customer proof points**
  - [**Zapier**](https://mintlify.com/customers/zapier): **3x faster** doc updates.
  - [**Layers**](https://mintlify.com/customers/layers): **40–60 hours** saved monthly.
  - [**PayPal**](https://mintlify.com/customers/paypal) and [**Perplexity**](https://mintlify.com/customers/perplexity): Evolved docs into full AI-native experiences, leading to broader adoption.
